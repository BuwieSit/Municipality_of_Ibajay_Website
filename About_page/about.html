Kasaysayan ng Ibajay

Ibajay is a historically rich municipality known for its deep religious and cultural roots. A significant figure in its history is Alejandro Melchor, a native of Ibajay, who was a civil engineer, mathematician, and Secretary of National Defense under President Manuel Quezon. Melchor is notably recognized for designing pontoon bridges that played a crucial role in the Allied Forces' success during World War II.

The town also holds an annual religious celebration, the Ibajay Ati-Ati Municipal and Devotional Fiesta, in honor of its patron saint, Sto. Niño. This tradition highlights the town's strong Catholic devotion and commemorates the discovery and enshrinement of the Holy Child’s image in the local church.

Vision

Ibajay envisions a progressive and sustainable community where economic growth, environmental protection, and cultural heritage go hand in hand. Through inclusive development, responsible governance, and community empowerment, the municipality strives to improve the quality of life for all, while contributing to the broader vision of a Progressive and Sustainable Aklan.

Mission

The mission of the province is to achieve its vision by preserving and promoting arts, culture, and tourism; advancing health, social services, and an inclusive environment; protecting ecological balance; fostering scientific and technological development; upholding moral values and accountability; promoting economic growth, food security, and social justice; ensuring equal employment and livelihood opportunities; strengthening disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation; maintaining peace and order; and ultimately improving the quality of life for the people of Aklan.
